## Pearson Supply 1 Demand A Scenario Assumptions

The simulation starts in January 1971.
ITER requires the following tritium: 100 g in 2027; 200 g in 2030; 800 g yearly from 2031 to 2034; 1100 g for 12 years starting in 2035.

Canadian Tritium
* Whan all Canadian units are operational, 1932 g of tritium are prduced each year. Production is based on the proportion of power from the units that are online.
* Sales occur at a rate of 100 g/year.
* The known schedule for Canadian CANDU operation from 2017 was used.
* Units from Bruce, Darlington, Pickering, and Gentilly 2 were used in the simulation.

Korean Tritium 
* When all four Wolsong units are operational, 780 g of tritium are produced each year. Production is based on the proportion of units that are online.
*	Wolsong units will begin shutdown in 2022 with the last units shutting down by 2030.
*	Tritium extraction will occur at a reduced rate until 2032.(This is accountedfor by keeping units 3 and 4 in the simulation until 2032).


## Abdou Scenario Assumptions

There are 18.5 kg of tritium in January 2003.
For an operational lifetime of 40 years, 45 - 55 actual years were used to account for periods of downtime. Older reactors are assumed to need more downtime.
Tritium extraction occurs during periods of downtime.

Canadian Tritium
* Tritium extraction occurs at a rate of 1.5 kg/year. Production is based on the proportion of units that are online.
* Units begin shutting down in 2025.
* Units include Bruce 1-8, Darlington 1-4, Pickering 1-8, Point Lepreau, and Gentilly 2.

Korean Tritium
* Production starts in 2007 with WTRF coming online.
* Tritium production factor is 0.2 kg/GWe based on operation availability from IAEA website.
* There is no refurbishment of these reactors.
